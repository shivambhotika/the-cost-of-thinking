‚ñ∂ CLICK HERE: "Wait, what even IS AI?" (5-minute explanation) ‚ñ∂

AI in one sentence: Teaching computers to find patterns in data so they can make predictions or decisions.

How it works (simple version):

  1. You show the computer millions of examples (books, images, conversations)
  2. It finds patterns (like "cats usually have pointy ears" or "this word usually comes after that word")
  3. When you ask it something new, it uses those patterns to respond

Why it needs so much power: Finding patterns in billions of examples requires trillions of calculations. More examples + more calculations = better patterns = smarter AI.

‚ñ∂ WHY is compute growing so fast? (Deep dive) ‚ñ∂

Three reasons:

  1. More data = better AI: Newer models are trained on more text, images, and videos.
    Example: GPT-3 saw 300 billion words. GPT-4 probably saw 10+ trillion.
  2. Bigger models = smarter AI: Models with more parameters can learn more complex patterns.
    Example: GPT-1 had 117M parameters. GPT-5 has ~3 trillion (25,000x more).
  3. The scaling law works: Research shows that 10x more compute reliably makes AI better.
    Every major AI lab has confirmed this pattern holds.
‚ö° KEY TAKEAWAY

The compute requirement is growing faster than Moore's Law (which says chip performance doubles every 2 years).

This means we can't just wait for better chips. We need more chips, more power, and more money to keep up.

PART 2: THE POWER INDEX

How Much Electricity Does This Require?

‚ö° THE BIG PICTURE

Main point: All those calculations need electricity. AI's power hunger is doubling every 5 years.

Why it matters: Energy infrastructure takes years to build. Countries that can't supply power can't compete in AI.

‚ñ∂ Wait, why does AI need SO MUCH power? ‚ñ∂

Simple explanation: Imagine you have 10,000 supercomputers doing calculations 24/7 for 3 months straight.

The math:

  • One NVIDIA H100 chip uses 700 watts (like 7 old lightbulbs)
  • A training cluster might have 10,000-25,000 chips
  • Total power draw: 7-18 megawatts (a small town)
  • Running for 3 months = 15-40 million kWh

Real example: Training GPT-4 likely used 50 megawatts for several months. That's 50,000 US homes.
